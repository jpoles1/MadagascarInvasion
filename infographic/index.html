Getting to Know Madagascar's Worst Invasive Species

Wild-Sage (Plant): Lantana Camara

  • Adaptable shrub that can colonize a variety of ecosystems.
  • Disrupts ecoystem succession and
  • Documented to have stalled the regeneration of rainforest for three decades.
  • Biological interventions have yet to show good efficacy
  • Mechanical removal requires constant control of regrowth

Chytrid (Fungus): Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis

  • Threatens Madagascar's >200 unique frog species.
  • First introduced in 2010, it was likely spread by the exotic pet trade.
  • Frogs are integral to ecosystem health, and may serve as a source of new antibiotics
  • Urgent need to track the spread of Chytrid, and the emergence of virulent strains
  • Maintenence of captive populations may save species and allow for drug research

Ship Rat (Mammal): Rattus rattus

  • Spread to Madagascar from India several hundred years ago.
  • Carries diseases of humans (Black Plague) and animals (toxoplasmosis)
  • Outcompetes native rodent species
  • Devours the eggs and young of native bird and reptile species
  • Eats and ruins food supplies
