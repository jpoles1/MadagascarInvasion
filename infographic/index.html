Getting to Know Madagascar's Worst Invasive Species

Chytrid (Fungus): Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis

  • Chytrid attacks Madagascar's >200 unique frog species.
  • Chytrid was introduced in 2010, it was likely spread by the exotic pet trade.
  • Chytrid may not be easily defeated, but we can fight
  • Monitoring of Chytrid and maintenence of captive populations could save Madagascar's frogs.

Wild-Sage (Plant): Lantana Camara

  • Variable shrub that can survive in many habitats.
  • Disrupts ecoystem succession: has been documented to have completely stalled the regeneration of rainforest for three decades.
  • Attempts have been made to control the spread of Lantana; however continual control of regrowth is needed

Ship Rat (Mammal): Rattus rattus

  • Spread to Madagascar from India several hundred years ago.
  • Carries both human diseases like the Black Plague and animal diseases like toxoplasmosis
  • Outcompetes native rodent species
  • Devours the eggs and young of native bird and reptile species
  • Eats and ruins food supplies
